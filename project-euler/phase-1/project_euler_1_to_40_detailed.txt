
PROJECT EULER (1–40)
DETAILED PROBLEM DESCRIPTIONS – FOR CODING
=========================================

NOTE:
-----
Each problem below includes:
• What the problem is asking
• A simple example (when helpful)
• What you are expected to code (logic-wise)

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 1 – Multiples of 3 or 5
-----------------------------------------
Find the sum of all natural numbers below 1000 that are divisible by 3 or 5.
Example: Below 10 → 3, 5, 6, 9 → sum = 23
You must loop numbers, check divisibility, and accumulate sum.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 2 – Even Fibonacci numbers
-----------------------------------------
Generate Fibonacci numbers and sum only the even-valued terms
whose value does not exceed 4 million.
Teaches sequence generation and conditional filtering.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 3 – Largest prime factor
-----------------------------------------
Find the largest prime factor of a large number (600851475143).
Requires factorization and optimization (checking up to √n).

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 4 – Largest palindrome product
-----------------------------------------
Find the largest palindrome made from the product of two 3-digit numbers.
Palindrome = reads same forward and backward.
Requires nested loops + palindrome check.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 5 – Smallest multiple
-----------------------------------------
Find the smallest positive number evenly divisible by all numbers from 1 to 20.
Best solved using LCM, not brute force.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 6 – Sum square difference
-----------------------------------------
Find the difference between:
(square of sum of first 100 numbers) and (sum of squares).
Math simplification encouraged.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 7 – 10001st prime
-----------------------------------------
Find the 10001st prime number.
Requires prime checking or sieve.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 8 – Largest product in a series
-----------------------------------------
Find the greatest product of 13 adjacent digits in a 1000-digit number.
Sliding window logic.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 9 – Special Pythagorean triplet
-----------------------------------------
Find a, b, c such that:
a² + b² = c² and a + b + c = 1000.
Return a × b × c.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 10 – Summation of primes
-----------------------------------------
Find the sum of all primes below 2 million.
Sieve of Eratosthenes strongly recommended.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 11 – Largest product in a grid
-----------------------------------------
Find the greatest product of four adjacent numbers
in a 20×20 grid (horizontal, vertical, diagonal).

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 12 – Highly divisible triangular number
-----------------------------------------
Find the first triangular number with over 500 divisors.
Requires divisor counting optimization.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 13 – Large sum
-----------------------------------------
Add 100 large (50-digit) numbers and return first 10 digits.
BigInt usage recommended.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 14 – Longest Collatz sequence
-----------------------------------------
For numbers below 1 million, find which produces the longest Collatz chain.
Use memoization for speed.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 15 – Lattice paths
-----------------------------------------
Count routes through a 20×20 grid moving only right or down.
Combinatorics or DP solution.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 16 – Power digit sum
-----------------------------------------
Find the sum of digits of 2¹⁰⁰⁰.
BigInt and digit handling.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 17 – Number letter counts
-----------------------------------------
Count letters used if numbers 1–1000 are written in words.
(Spaces and hyphens ignored.)

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 18 – Maximum path sum I
-----------------------------------------
Find the maximum total from top to bottom of a triangle of numbers.
Classic dynamic programming.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 19 – Counting Sundays
-----------------------------------------
Count how many Sundays fell on the first of the month during 20th century.
Date logic without libraries.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 20 – Factorial digit sum
-----------------------------------------
Find sum of digits of 100!.
BigInt + digit extraction.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 21 – Amicable numbers
-----------------------------------------
Find sum of all amicable numbers under 10000.
Requires divisor sums.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 22 – Names scores
-----------------------------------------
Sort names alphabetically, multiply name score by position.
String handling + sorting.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 23 – Non-abundant sums
-----------------------------------------
Find numbers that cannot be written as sum of two abundant numbers.
Set logic + optimization.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 24 – Lexicographic permutations
-----------------------------------------
Find the millionth permutation of digits 0–9.
Factorial number system approach.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 25 – 1000-digit Fibonacci number
-----------------------------------------
Find index of first Fibonacci number with 1000 digits.
BigInt Fibonacci.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 26 – Reciprocal cycles
-----------------------------------------
Find value of d < 1000 for which 1/d has longest recurring cycle.
Long division simulation.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 27 – Quadratic primes
-----------------------------------------
Find quadratic formula producing maximum primes.
Brute force with pruning.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 28 – Number spiral diagonals
-----------------------------------------
Sum diagonals in a 1001×1001 spiral.
Pattern recognition, no grid needed.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 29 – Distinct powers
-----------------------------------------
Count distinct values of a^b for 2 ≤ a,b ≤ 100.
Use Set + BigInt.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 30 – Digit fifth powers
-----------------------------------------
Find numbers equal to sum of fifth powers of their digits.
Example: 4150 = 4⁵ + 1⁵ + 5⁵ + 0⁵.
Determine reasonable upper bound first.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 31 – Coin sums
-----------------------------------------
How many ways can £2 be made using UK coins?
Order does NOT matter.
Dynamic programming problem.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 32 – Pandigital products
-----------------------------------------
Find products whose multiplicand/multiplier/product
use digits 1–9 exactly once.
Example: 39 × 186 = 7254.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 33 – Digit cancelling fractions
-----------------------------------------
Find non-trivial fractions where cancelling digits
incorrectly still gives correct result.
Multiply all and reduce final fraction.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 34 – Digit factorials
-----------------------------------------
Find numbers equal to sum of factorial of digits.
Example: 145 = 1! + 4! + 5!.
Precompute factorials.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 35 – Circular primes
-----------------------------------------
A prime that remains prime under all digit rotations.
Example: 197 → 197, 971, 719.
Use sieve + rotations.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 36 – Double-base palindromes
-----------------------------------------
Find numbers < 1 million palindromic in base 10 and base 2.
Example: 585.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 37 – Truncatable primes
-----------------------------------------
Primes that remain prime when digits removed left-to-right and right-to-left.
Requires filtering and primality checking.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 38 – Pandigital multiples
-----------------------------------------
Concatenate multiples of a number to form 1–9 pandigital.
Find maximum such number.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 39 – Integer right triangles
-----------------------------------------
For p ≤ 1000, find perimeter with most right-angle triangles.
Nested loops with constraints.

-----------------------------------------
PROBLEM 40 – Champernowne’s constant
-----------------------------------------
Construct fractional constant by concatenating integers.
Find product of digits at given positions.

-----------------------------------------
END OF PHASE 1 (1–40)
